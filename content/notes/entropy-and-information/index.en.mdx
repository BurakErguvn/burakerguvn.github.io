The question on my mind is simple — how does the *information content* of a
variable depend on its probability? Shannon's answer:

$$
H(X) = -\sum_{x} p(x) \log_2 p(x)
$$

Intuitively: the more surprising an event, the more "information" it carries.
The `log_2` base gives the result in *bits*; that's why each symbol of a 2-symbol
alphabet carries exactly 1 bit (under equal probabilities).
